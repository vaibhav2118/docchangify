How to Split a Video into Equal Segments

  1. Click Browse Video or drag and drop your MP4, MOV, or AVI file into the upload area.
  2. Enter a split duration in seconds (e.g., 10 for 10-second clips), or pick a preset.
  3. Click Split Video. FFmpeg.wasm will process the file locally in your browser.
  4. Preview each generated clip, then download individually or all clips as a ZIP file.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this upload my video to a server?

No. The entire process runs in your browser using WebAssembly (FFmpeg.wasm). Your video file never leaves your computer.

What happens if the video length isn't divisible by the split duration?

The last clip will contain the remaining footage — for example, if your video is 23 seconds and you split every 5 seconds, the 5th clip will be 3 seconds long.

What video formats are supported?

MP4, MOV, and AVI are officially supported. Output clips are always saved as MP4 (H.264) for maximum compatibility.

Why does the first load take a moment?

FFmpeg.wasm is a large WebAssembly module (~20 MB) that needs to download once. After that, everything runs locally and no internet is required.
